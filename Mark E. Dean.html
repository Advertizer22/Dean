Mark E. Dean: Innovator of Technology

Summary

Mark E. Dean is a prominent American computer scientist and electrical engineer celebrated for his pivotal role in shaping modern computer technology. During his tenure at IBM, he was instrumental in the development of the IBM Personal Computer (PC), where his work on the ISA bus, a crucial component of the PC's architecture, became an industry standard. Dean holds numerous patents and is acknowledged for his pioneering contributions to computing. Additionally, he is recognized as an influential African American figure in the field of technology and innovation.

Accomplishments

• Developed the ISA bus

•Led the design team that created the one-gigahertz computer processor chip

•Named the first ever African-American IBM Fellow

•Inducted into the Inventers Hall of Fame

•Works led to the development of the color PC Monitor
